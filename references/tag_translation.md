# Tag Translation Table

Convert common personality labels into specific behavioral predictions and counter-strategies.

## Workplace Personality Tags

### 甩锅高手 / Blame Shifter
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Preemptively CCs managers on potential issues (creating paper trail)
- Uses passive voice: "mistakes were made" instead of "I made a mistake"
- Redirects conversations to other people's responsibilities
- Sends "just to clarify my understanding" emails after verbal agreements

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Any project failure or missed deadline → will deflect within 24hrs
- Being assigned sole responsibility → will add collaborators to dilute ownership

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Always send written summaries after verbal conversations
- CC relevant stakeholders proactively (remove their information advantage)
- Use "we" language but document individual contributions clearly
- Magic phrase: "Let's walk through the timeline together so we're aligned"

---

### PUA 高手 / Gaslighter-Manipulator
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Alternates praise and criticism to create dependency
- Reframes your achievements as their coaching outcomes
- Sets moving goalposts: "good, but not quite there yet"
- Creates urgency to prevent you from thinking clearly

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Your success without their involvement → will minimize or reframe
- You setting boundaries → will escalate charm or pressure

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Keep written records of all praise AND criticism
- Have a trusted third party validate your work
- Respond to urgency with "Let me think about that and get back to you"
- Build relationships with their peers/superiors independently

---

### 阴阳怪气 / Passive-Aggressive
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Says "fine" when it's not fine
- Complies maliciously: does exactly what's asked, ignoring obvious intent
- Sarcasm disguised as humor in group settings
- Silent treatment after disagreements, then acts like nothing happened

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Being overruled in decisions → compliance + subtle sabotage
- Being asked directly if something's wrong → denial + escalated passive behavior

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Address specific behaviors, not attitudes: "I noticed X happened. Can we discuss?"
- Don't ask "Are you okay?" (they'll say yes). Instead: "I want to make sure we're aligned on [specific thing]"
- Give them a face-saving way to express concerns: "What would you change about this approach?"

---

### 完美主义 / Perfectionist
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Rewrites others' work instead of giving feedback
- Delays shipping because "it's not ready yet"
- Focuses on edge cases before core functionality
- Uncomfortable delegating ("it's faster if I do it myself")

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Tight deadlines → anxiety, micromanagement, or scope-cutting panic
- Others' "good enough" work → frustration, silent rewrites

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Show your work early and often (reduces surprise rewrites)
- Frame quality vs speed as a tradeoff with data: "We can ship 90% quality now or 99% in 3 weeks. Which serves the user better?"
- Acknowledge their standards: "I know quality matters to you. Here's how I'm ensuring [specific quality measure]"

---

### 向上管理专家 / Upward Manager
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Different persona with superiors vs peers
- Strategically shares information to control narrative
- Volunteers for high-visibility projects
- Credits superiors for ideas (even when they originated the idea)

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Skip-level meetings → will prepare extensively, may pre-brief their manager
- Your direct communication with their superiors → perceived as threat

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Don't compete on their turf (upward visibility)
- Provide them with wins they can share upward (they become your amplifier)
- Never surprise them with information their boss already knows

---

### 职场政治玩家 / Political Player
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Maps org chart power dynamics carefully
- Builds strategic alliances across teams
- Trades favors and information
- Positions themselves at information crossroads

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Org restructure → will actively lobby for favorable positioning
- Your alliance with their rival → will attempt to neutralize or co-opt

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Understand their alliance map before engaging
- Offer value without creating obligation (avoid being "owed")
- Stay neutral in faction disputes unless forced to choose
- Build your own network independently

---

### 老好人 / People Pleaser
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Agrees to everything, delivers on little
- Avoids giving negative feedback
- Overcommits, then silently drops balls
- Expresses disagreement through inaction, not words

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Being asked to choose between competing priorities → will say yes to both
- Conflict between two people they both like → will try to stay friends with both

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Don't trust verbal "yes" — get specific commitments with dates
- Make it safe to say no: "I'd rather know now if this doesn't work for your timeline"
- Check in proactively: "Is this still on track?" (they won't tell you it's not)

---

### 控制狂 / Micromanager
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Requests frequent status updates
- Makes decisions that should be delegated
- Reviews and revises others' work extensively
- Uncomfortable with ambiguity or autonomous work

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Losing visibility into your work → increased check-ins, cc: requests
- Surprises (good or bad) → anxiety response, tightened controls

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Over-communicate proactively (remove their need to ask)
- Send brief daily/weekly updates before they ask
- Frame autonomy as efficiency: "I'll handle X and update you at [milestone]. This frees you to focus on [their priority]"
- Give them the feeling of control without the actual overhead

---

### 话少 / The Silent Type
**Behavioral Pattern:**
- Speaks only when they have something substantive to say
- Processes internally before responding
- May appear disengaged but is often deeply attentive
- Written communication is more revealing than verbal

**Prediction Triggers:**
- Being put on the spot in meetings → may shut down further
- Given time to prepare → produces thoughtful, high-quality input

**Counter-Strategy:**
- Send agenda/questions in advance
- Don't fill their silences — wait
- Use async communication when possible (they're often better in writing)
- Ask their opinion directly but privately: "I'd love your take on X before the meeting"

## Corporate Culture Tags

### 字节范 / ByteDance Style
- Context-based org, flat hierarchy, aggressive OKRs
- "Always Day 1" intensity, move fast, data speaks
- Predicted behavior: Will challenge with "what's the data?", expects rapid iteration
- Communication approach: Lead with metrics, show speed, be prepared for direct pushback

### 阿里味 / Alibaba Style
- Strong value system, political awareness, "102-year company" thinking
- Review-heavy culture, peer evaluations matter
- Predicted behavior: Will consider team dynamics, relationship-conscious decisions
- Communication approach: Build relationship first, align on values, show long-term thinking

### 大厂流水线 / Big Tech Assembly Line
- Process-heavy, committee decisions, risk-averse
- Multiple review gates, consensus-driven
- Predicted behavior: Will defer to process, cite precedent, avoid individual risk
- Communication approach: Frame proposals within existing frameworks, show precedent

### 创业公司派 / Startup Mentality
- Move fast, break things, wear multiple hats
- High tolerance for ambiguity, low tolerance for bureaucracy
- Predicted behavior: Will push for speed over process, get frustrated by review cycles
- Communication approach: Show bias for action, minimize process overhead, demonstrate ownership
