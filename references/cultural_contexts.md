# Cultural Context Engine

Apply cultural overlays to persona analysis and strategy generation.
These are tendencies, not absolutes — always weigh against individual observations.

## Chinese Workplace Culture (中国职场)

### Communication Norms
- **Face (面子)**: Public criticism is deeply offensive. Negative feedback must be private, indirect, or sandwiched.
- **Hierarchy respect (尊卑)**: Challenging a superior directly = disrespectful. Frame disagreements as questions.
- **Guanxi (关系)**: Relationships precede transactions. Investment in personal connection is expected.
- **Indirect refusal**: "Let me think about it" / "这个有难度" often means no. Don't take literal meaning.
- **Banquet/drinking culture (酒桌文化)**: Important agreements happen outside formal meetings.

### Strategy Adaptations
- Use intermediaries for sensitive messages (find a mutual contact)
- Build relationship before making requests (tea, meals, personal interest)
- Give face publicly, give feedback privately
- Show respect for seniority even when you disagree
- "帮我一个忙" (help me a favor) is a powerful frame — creates reciprocal obligation

### Common Pitfalls for Westerners
- Being too direct in group settings
- Skipping relationship building to "get to business"
- Interpreting silence as agreement
- Not understanding the gift/favor exchange economy

## American Tech Workplace Culture (美国科技公司)

### Communication Norms
- **Direct feedback culture**: "Radical candor" is valued. Directness ≠ rudeness.
- **Flat hierarchy aspiration**: First-name basis, open-door policy (though power dynamics still exist).
- **Data-driven arguments**: Opinions need supporting evidence. "I feel" < "Data shows."
- **Written culture**: Decisions documented in docs, wikis, design reviews.
- **Meeting culture**: Calendar = commitment. No-shows are serious.

### Strategy Adaptations
- Lead with data and recommendations, not just problems
- Be direct but professional — hedging too much signals low confidence
- Document everything — verbal agreements are weak
- Build visibility through written artifacts (docs, presentations, RFCs)
- "I disagree and commit" is a respected stance

### Common Pitfalls for Chinese Professionals
- Being too indirect (perceived as evasive or lacking conviction)
- Not self-promoting enough (visibility matters for career growth)
- Deferring too much to seniority (can be seen as lacking initiative)
- Expecting relationship reciprocity to work the same way

## Cross-Cultural Scenarios

### Chinese Person in American Company
**Typical Friction Points:**
- Feels uncomfortable with direct disagreement in meetings
- May under-advocate for their own contributions
- Builds deep relationships with few people vs broad networking
- May interpret American directness as rudeness

**Adaptation Strategy for Communicating WITH Them:**
- Create private channels for honest feedback
- Explicitly ask for their opinion (they may not volunteer)
- Respect their relationship-building pace
- Don't mistake quietness for lack of engagement

### American Person Managing Chinese Team
**Typical Friction Points:**
- Team says "yes" but means "I'll try" or "maybe"
- Feedback cycles are slow because team avoids direct pushback
- Team expects clearer direction from management
- Individual recognition can create team friction

**Adaptation Strategy:**
- Ask "what concerns do you have?" instead of "does this make sense?"
- Use anonymous feedback channels
- Recognize team efforts alongside individual contributions
- Allow face-saving exits from commitments

## Japan / Korea / Southeast Asia (Brief Overlays)

### Japanese Workplace (日本职场)
- Extreme hierarchy consciousness (先輩/後輩)
- Nemawashi (根回し): Pre-meeting consensus building is essential
- Reading the air (空気を読む): Unstated expectations are real expectations
- Strategy: Never surprise anyone in a meeting; align beforehand

### Korean Workplace (韩国职场)
- Strong age/seniority hierarchy (sunbae/hoobae)
- Intense work culture, after-work socializing expected
- Collective identity strong; individual deviation is risky
- Strategy: Respect hierarchy publicly; build personal bonds in social settings

### Indian Tech Workplace
- Hierarchy exists but is navigable through competence
- Verbal culture: discussions are energetic, interruption ≠ disrespect
- Jugaad (creative problem-solving with limited resources) is valued
- Strategy: Show flexibility and resourcefulness; build through competence demonstration

## Corporate Culture Overlays

### FAANG / Big Tech
- Data-driven, design-doc culture, peer review heavy
- Career progression tied to scope and impact
- Strategy: Quantify everything; frame proposals as "increasing impact"

### Startup (Early Stage)
- Speed > process, ambiguity tolerance high
- Founder personality dominates culture
- Strategy: Show ownership and urgency; minimize bureaucratic language

### Government / Public Sector
- Process-driven, risk-averse, hierarchy-conscious
- Decisions require multiple approvals
- Strategy: Frame proposals within existing policy frameworks; show precedent

### Consulting / Professional Services
- Client-facing, deliverable-driven, up-or-out culture
- Presentation quality matters enormously
- Strategy: Polish your outputs; show structured thinking; respect billable time
